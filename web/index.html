EAAA Curriculum — Where Avoidance Lives

Cooper Color Code Tier C

White → Yellow (relaxed alert) → Orange (threat identified) → Red (action). Widely adopted in military, LE, and aviation. No civilian RCT linking color-code training to victimization reduction.

Stay Alert, Stay Alive Tier C

U.S. military force protection: vigilance, route planning, low profile, escape prioritization. Doctrinally correct. Outcome data is institutional, not peer-reviewed for civilians.

EAAA "Assess" Unit Tier A+

Trains faster danger detection from acquaintances, problem-solving to reduce perpetrator advantage. 50–57% rape reduction in RCT and real-world replication. Closest validated proxy for "sensed danger → took action."

NCVS Verbal/Passive Resistance Tier A

Assault victims 65+ using verbal/passive strategies only: 57% less likely to be injured vs. physical resistance or no action (Bachman et al. 2022).
